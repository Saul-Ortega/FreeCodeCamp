Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

The Creator Of Unix And C Programming Language

Time line of Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie's life:

  • 1941 - Born in Bronxville, New York.
  • 1963 - Earns a bachelors's degree in phisics.
  • 1967 - Starts working at Bell Laboratories where he participates in various projects like Multics.
  • 1968 - Obtains a doctorate in mathematics.
  • 1969 - Starts developing Unix which is a multitasks and multiusers operating system.
  • 1970s - Begins developing the C programming language which at that time was considered as a high-level language compared to Assembler.
  • 1983 - Gets the Turing Award with Kenneth Lane Thompson for his development of the theory of generic operating systems and its implementarion in Unix.
  • 1990s - Dennis was transferred to Luccent Technologies and created Plan 9 and Inferno operating systems as well as the Limbo programming language.
  • 1998 - Gets the United States Medal of Technology for his contributions in important technologies.
  • 2007 - Retires from Lucent as head of the Systems Software Research Department.
  • 2011 - Dies at the age of 70 years due to cancer.
